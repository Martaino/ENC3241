# Instructions

## How to Cut a Video in Premiere Pro
What you will need in this tutorial:  
-	A Project in Premiere Pro.
-	A video imported in that project.

This is what your Premiere Pro Should look like before starting:  

<img width="975" height="523" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/55b0cf5c-8a30-4230-9987-5f256f5c0c99" />

###### Figure 1. Default Workspace in Premiere with an imported video. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman) 
Instructions
Step 1: Drag your video onto the timeline.  

 <img width="975" height="209" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/aece29c8-4ee4-421e-b3bb-9e23503ada12" />

###### Figure 2. Project and Timeline Windows in Step 1. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 2: Select the Razor Tool.  

<img width="690" height="209" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cfba849e-df65-4d2d-b48b-502f29fb4772" />

###### Figure 3. Razor Tool Location. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 3: Watch the video in the Program Window, by clicking on the play icon. 

<img width="975" height="523" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3a75c578-a8f2-4da8-8213-e9a4ea79ef5e" />

###### Figure 4. Program Window location and play icon location. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 4: Find the segments of the video you want to cut out.

Step 5: Using the Razor Tool, click on the start of the segment you want to cut out. 

<img width="864" height="283" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a00242c9-ba0b-4423-9861-1a2e6004d7f6" />

###### Figure 5. First Cut Example. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 6: Using the Razor Tool, click on the end of the segment you want to cut out.

<img width="898" height="295" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3be4f889-4ca1-4bcb-861e-ab5dc88cec9e" />

###### Figure 6. Second Cut Example. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 7: Select the Selection Tool.

<img width="895" height="296" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cdc07b4a-fa34-49f1-9ddf-4be819fa34dc" />

###### Figure 7. Selection Tool Location. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 8: Select the new segment cut out.

<img width="896" height="293" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3a6232cb-7ba9-4b52-b129-66e97ba11f94" />

###### Figure 8. Selection of Segment Example. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 9: Delete that segment.

<img width="897" height="297" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/141fa501-d870-4a35-b3d6-89da5a56cecb" />

###### Figure 9. Deletion of Segment Example. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 10: Right-Click on the empty space where the deleted segment was. 

<img width="898" height="297" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fc66128c-7f6f-4041-8beb-64f6b590e780" />

###### Figure 10. Right-Click Example and Ripple Delete Location. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 11: Click on Ripple Delete.

<img width="898" height="296" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c9d80840-f0af-4871-b28f-bc145d26abba" />

###### Figure 11. Results of All 11 Steps. (Screenshot by Martin Fanjul Guzman)
Step 12: Repeat these steps until your clip is cut fully.
